## Medical Appointments

### Scheduling a new appointment
- They will ask: patient name, date of birth, insurance info, reason for visit
- Typical hold time: 1-3 minutes
- If new patient: expect longer call, they may ask for address and pharmacy
- Common phrase: "The next available is..." — this means their earliest opening

### Rescheduling an appointment
- Have ready: current appointment date, preferred new date/time
- They may say: "Let me check availability" — this means brief hold
- If no slots available, ask: "What's the soonest opening?"
- Common phrase: "We have a cancellation on..." — this is good, take it if it works

### Canceling an appointment
- Some offices charge a cancellation fee if less than 24 hours
- Simply state: "I need to cancel my appointment on [date]"
- They may ask the reason — you do NOT have to give one
- Say: "Personal reasons" if you prefer not to explain

## Insurance Calls

### Checking coverage
- Have ready: member ID, group number, date of birth
- Expect: automated menu first, then hold for representative
- Average hold time: 5-15 minutes (this is normal)
- They will verify identity first before sharing info
- Common phrase: "Your plan covers..." followed by percentage or copay amount

### Filing a claim dispute
- Have ready: claim number, date of service, provider name
- Be specific: "I'm calling about claim number [X] from [date]"
- They may transfer you — this is normal, you may need to re-explain
- Ask for a reference number before hanging up

## Utility Companies

### Reporting an issue
- Have ready: account number, service address
- For emergencies (gas leak, power line down): say "emergency" immediately
- For billing questions: your last bill amount and date
- Common phrase: "I'll create a ticket" — this means they're logging your issue

### Setting up or canceling service
- Have ready: move-in/move-out date, new/old address, account number
- They may try to upsell — you can say "No thank you, just the basic service"
- Ask: "Is there a confirmation number?" — always get one

## Phone Call Anxiety — What's Normal

### Normal things that happen during calls:
- Hold music (sometimes loud) — this is normal, they haven't hung up
- Silence for 10-30 seconds — they're typing or looking something up
- Being transferred — common, especially for insurance and government
- Being asked to repeat yourself — the connection may be bad
- Automated menus — press numbers as prompted, "0" often reaches a person
- "Can you hold?" — say "yes" and wait, they will come back

### If you get overwhelmed:
- It is okay to say: "I need a moment" — they will wait
- It is okay to say: "Can I call back?" — they will say yes
- It is okay to hang up — you can always call again
- Deep breath between sentences is normal and not awkward

### Common confusing phrases decoded:
- "Bear with me" = "Please wait a moment"
- "I'll look into that" = "I'm searching for the answer now"
- "Let me pull up your account" = "I'm typing your info, wait 10-30 seconds"
- "Is there anything else I can help you with?" = "We're almost done, this is the ending"
- "Have a good one" = "Goodbye" — you can say "Thank you, bye"
- "For quality assurance" = "This call is being recorded, this is normal"
- "Your call is important to us" = "You're still on hold, please wait"
